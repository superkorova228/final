VINCENT LAW

Perhaps the name for this character was chosen blindly. But "Ergo Proxy" is full of coincidences that, upon closer inspection, reveal themselves to be both meaningful and ambiguous.

The name Vincent is associated with the patron saint of winemaking, and its origin is linked to the word "wine" (vino). The name Vincent itself is derived from the Latin vinco, meaning "to conquer, overcome, surpass"; vincens (from the root vincent), meaning "conquering" (present active participle). The surname Law translates as "law," "court," "right," or "rule." Accordingly, the full name can be translated as follows: Vincent Law – "conquering the law," "overcoming the law"; or, alternatively, "surpassing the law," "one who is above the law."

Some details in Vincent Law's appearance bring to mind Vincent Valentine, the hero of the famous game "Final Fantasy VII" (as well as the film "Advent Children" and the game "Dirge of Cerberus").

PINO

Pino herself and her name play on the character Pinocchio, with whom she shares some physical similarities (Pino looks like a doll) and a common story: turning into a human. The name "Pino" sounds like "piano" - the name of the musical instrument she plays... if there is a piano nearby, and in its absence, she plays the melodica, which, however, is only half related to the piano and belongs to the keyboard-wind instruments.

In addition, PINO is an operating system platform for humanoid robots.

MONADA

Monad (from the Greek monas, genitive monados – unit, one) is a philosophical term used in a number of philosophical systems to denote the constitutive elements or units of being.

The monad is one of the fundamental concepts of Leibniz's philosophy (Monadology, 1714). He considered the monad to be a simple, closed, active, and thus changing spiritual substance. Monads, endowed with the capacity for distinct perception, are called souls. The rational soul of man is the monad-spirit. According to Leibniz, the entire world is reflected in the monad; in the form of individuality, it contains within itself, as it were, an embryo of infinity.

Each Proxy's name has a specific meaning, but each Proxy is also a Messenger. Who was Monad if the one she sought (and who sought her) was the Messenger of Death? Perhaps Monad Proxy is the Messenger of Love.
